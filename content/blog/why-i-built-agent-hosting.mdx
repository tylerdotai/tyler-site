I've been running AI agents in production for over a year now. Every week, I'm reminded of the same painful truth: **deploying agents is still harder than it should be**.

## The Problem

Most "agent platforms" are either:

1. **Too complex** — Kubernetes, Docker, YAML configs, environment variables everywhere. By the time you deploy your first agent, you've lost a day.
2. **Too restrictive** — You can only use their pre-built agents, their specific tools, their proprietary runtime. Goodbye flexibility.
3. **Too expensive** — Enterprise pricing that makes sense for a Fortune 500, not for indie builders or small teams.

## First Principles

What do you actually need to deploy an AI agent?

- A way to give it **tools** (functions it can call)
- A way to give it **memory** (context that persists)
- A way to **expose it** (webhook, API, chat interface)
- A way to **monitor it** (logs, errors, usage)

That's it. Everything else is ceremony.

## My Approach

agent-hosting is built around these four primitives. You define your agent in a simple config file, wire up your tools, and hit deploy. 

```typescript
const agent = createAgent({
  model: 'gpt-4',
  tools: [weatherTool, calculator],
  memory: persistentMemory(),
})

export agent
```

The platform handles the rest: scaling, logging, error recovery, monitoring.

## Why This Matters

We're entering the age of the agent workforce. But that workforce can't scale if deployment is a pain. Every hour spent wrestling with infrastructure is an hour not spent improving the agent's logic, UX, or business value.

I built agent-hosting because I wanted it to exist. Now it does.
