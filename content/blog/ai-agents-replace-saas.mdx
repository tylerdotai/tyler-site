I said this at a meetup last month and someone literally laughed out loud. Fair. It sounds extreme. But let me walk through why I believe it.

## What SaaS Actually Does

Strip away the marketing, and most SaaS products do one of these things:

1. **Collect data** — CRM, analytics, forms, surveys
2. **Automate workflows** — email sequences, task assignment, approvals
3. **Provide access** — dashboards, reports, files, records
4. **Facilitate communication** — chat, video, comments, notifications

Now ask yourself: which of these can't an AI agent do better?

## The Compression

When you replace a SaaS tool with an agent:

- **No UI to build or maintain** — the agent IS the interface
- **No data model to design** — agents work with natural language
- **No workflow builder** — just tell the agent what you want
- **No onboarding** — no tutorials, no empty states, no "welcome to [Product]"

This isn't science fiction. It's happening now, quietly, in a thousand side projects and a few serious production deployments.

## The Timeline

My bet:

- **Year 1-2:** Low-complexity SaaS gets eaten first. Form builders, simple CRMs, basic automation tools.
- **Year 3-4:** Mid-market tools. The Zapier/Make tier, project management, simple analytics.
- **Year 5:** Enterprise software starts feeling the pressure.

## Why Not Sooner?

Two reasons:

1. **Trust** — enterprises need SOC2, HIPAA, GDPR compliance. Agents are new. This takes time.
2. **Complexity** — some workflows are genuinely complex. Agents make mistakes. Humans are still in the loop for high-stakes decisions.
