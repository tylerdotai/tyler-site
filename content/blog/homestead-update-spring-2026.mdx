It's that time of year again. Time to assess what lived, what died, and what I forgot to water.

## The Garden: A Study in Optimism

Last year I tried to grow tomatoes in the Texas spring heat. They produced exactly seven cherry tomatoes before giving up. This year I'm trying a different approach: smaller pots, afternoon shade, and actual watering consistency (revolutionary, I know).

**Status:** Hopeful but guarded.

The herbs are doing fine — rosemary, thyme, oregano. These plants could survive a nuclear winter. I respect that about them.

## The Chickens

We expanded the flock to 12 hens and 2 roosters (Spencer named them "Sir Clucks-a-Lot" and "Nugget" — I don't make the rules).

Egg production is up. We're giving away eggs to neighbors. This is peak homestead energy.

## Spencer's First Coding Project

Spencer (7) has been watching me code for a while. Last week he asked if he could make "a game where a dinosaur jumps over cacti."

So we built it. In Scratch. Together.

He's obsessed. Asks to "do coding" every morning before school. This might be the most satisfying thing I've built all year — and I mean that completely seriously.

## The Takeaway

 homesteading is just distributed systems management with worse tooling and more variables.

The skills transfer: iteration, monitoring, failure analysis, optimization. Highly recommend.
